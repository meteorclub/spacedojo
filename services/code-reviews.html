As you already know, fine tuning your Meteor application is a vital step in your growth cycle. Sometimes it can be simple: turn on Oplog support and add a few backend servers. Other times, you need to go deeper and pick code apart. Ultimately, you may need to take a look at denormalizing, changing your database structure, or adding microservices.

Nonetheless, it is vital to set time aside, get baseline measurements, and understand where performance problems may occur—long before you get that 2 AM email that everything is down.

Here's one example...

One of my clients, WorkLife, is a web and mobile app that helps people run more productive meetings by offering real time collaboration features. Some of their customers include Toyota, Hubspot and Zendesk.

As I began optimizing their inner workings for maximum efficiency, I identified that one of their publications had the second highest subscribe rate across the application, with an average response time of 2171ms.

Essentially, the problem was that WorkLife's database was doing more work than it had to. After identifying the problem in the database, I added a simple line of code to tell the database to add an index. This small change led to a 15X performance increase.
